# Attribution

## Methods

CoreLog Vision implements established core-image lithology CV. The methods are attributable to:

- **Baraboshkin, E. E. et al. (2020).** Deep convolutions for in-depth automated rock typing. *Computers &
  Geosciences*, 135, 104330. — CNN rock typing from core photographs.
- **Alzubaidi, F. et al. (2021).** Automated lithology classification from drill core images using CNNs. *J.
  Petroleum Science and Engineering*, 197, 107933.
- **LeCun, Y., Bengio, Y. & Hinton, G. (2015).** Deep learning. *Nature*, 521, 436–444. — the CNN backbone.
- **Haralick, R. M. et al. (1973).** Textural features for image classification. *IEEE T-SMC*, 3(6), 610–621. — the
  classical texture-feature baseline.
- **Ojala, T. et al. (2002).** Local binary patterns. *IEEE T-PAMI*, 24(7). — texture features.
- **Hendrycks, D. & Gimpel, K. (2017).** A baseline for detecting out-of-distribution examples. *ICLR*. — the OOD idea.

Full bibliography in `frontend/src/data/citations.ts` and the in-app Methodology page.

## Data

All core-tray images are **synthetic** — procedural per-lithology textures generated by `frontend/src/cv/textures.ts`
(a base colour + grain + banding/veining + seeded noise). No real drill-core photography is used or redistributed.
The `C-UNIFORM` and `C-SHARP` cases are closed-form analytic controls.
